## Analysis Summary

| Outcome family | Main finding | Supports which story |
|---|---|---|
| **Value estimation** | Little/no clear condition difference. PP is descriptively lowest-error for correlation estimation; CP/PP are descriptively lower-error for slope estimation, but model effects are not clear. | PlotWise did not clearly improve direct numerical value estimation. |
| **Add-points** | Correct-direction results show a weak/marginal lecture × practice pattern; PP tends to be strongest and CP weakest. The stricter reached-negative-r check shows a stronger interaction signal. | Practice may work better when participants first see PlotWise modeled in lecture; practice alone may not be enough. |
| **Slope preservation in add-points** | Slope preservation does not follow the same pattern as correct direction. CP/PP can preserve slope relatively well even when correct-direction performance differs. | Correlation control and slope preservation may be partly separate skills. |
| **Interest / motivation** | Interest scales show no strong condition differences. Practice motivation item is descriptive only: around 61% wanted more practice. | No significant difference from PlotWise conditions. |
| **Conceptual open response** | PlotWise lecture conditions, especially PC and PP, gave stronger conceptual explanations. This remains after controlling for prior knowledge. | PlotWise lecture may help students articulate conceptual relationships between slope and correlation. |
| **Choose-r recognition** | Close to chance and fairly flat across conditions. |  |
